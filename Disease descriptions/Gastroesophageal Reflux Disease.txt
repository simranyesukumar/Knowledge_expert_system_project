Gastroesophageal reflux (GER) happens when your stomach contents come back up into your esophagus. 
Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD) is a more severe and long-lasting condition in which GER causes repeated symptoms or leads to complications over time

Symptoms:

Common signs and symptoms of GERD include:
A burning sensation in your chest (heartburn), usually after eating, which might be worse at night or while lying down.
Backwash (regurgitation) of food or sour liquid.
Upper abdominal or chest pain.
Trouble swallowing (dysphagia)
Sensation of a lump in your throat.
